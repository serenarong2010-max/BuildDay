Why Schools Should Participate

Real skills for the real world, taught through play

Build Day is an educational hackathon designed to introduce students to game development through Roblox Studio. Students are guided through hands-on building, simple scripting, and collaborative design challenges that make learning feel playful while still delivering real academic value.

The event is intentionally structured for schools. Teachers can support the learning goals, mentors handle the technical guidance, and every activity is designed to strengthen creativity, collaboration, communication, and problem-solving in a way students understand immediately.

For schools, the value is in the learning design. Build Day channels student excitement for Roblox into an experience that supports digital citizenship, spatial reasoning, computational thinking, and a growth mindset. It is playful, but it is also purposeful.

💡

Creative Thinking

Students learn to transform ideas into interactive experiences. Game design encourages imagination, innovation, and out-of-the-box thinking that applies to all subjects. Through the creative process of building worlds, designing challenges, and crafting narratives, students practice turning ideas into something real.

That creative confidence carries into writing, art, engineering, and entrepreneurship because students see that they can design, revise, and improve their own work.

🧩

Problem-Solving

Building games teaches logical thinking and debugging. Students learn to break complex challenges into manageable steps and develop persistence when facing obstacles. Every game they build requires them to think systematically: planning level progression, balancing difficulty, testing mechanics, and iterating based on feedback.

Those are the same problem-solving habits used in mathematics, science, and real-world decision making, which is why the experience fits so naturally into school learning goals.

👥

Teamwork

Collaborative projects teach communication, delegation, and shared responsibility. Students learn that the best creations come from combining different strengths and perspectives. Whether working in pairs or larger groups, students practice articulating ideas, listening to feedback, and negotiating design decisions.

Educators also see stronger classroom dynamics because the event gives students a structured chance to lead, listen, and celebrate collective success.

What is Build Day?

An educational adventure in game development

🎮

A Learning Experience Disguised as Fun

Build Day is a full-day educational hackathon where students learn to create their own games in Roblox Studio. While they're having fun building obstacle courses and puzzles, they're actually developing computational thinking, spatial reasoning, and digital literacy skills that are essential for success in the 21st century.

📚

Aligned with Educational Standards

Our curriculum aligns with CSTA Computer Science Standards and ISTE Standards for Students. Students practice mathematics through game physics, develop language arts skills through storytelling and documentation, and learn scientific method through testing and iteration.

🎯

Structured for Classroom Success

Everything is organized for easy implementation: pre-built lesson plans, teacher guides, mentor support, and all the technical infrastructure. You just bring the students—we handle the rest. No prior coding or game development experience required for teachers!

During Build Day, students work in a supportive, collaborative environment guided by experienced mentors who understand both game development and education. The day is structured to maximize learning while keeping engagement high through hands-on creation, peer collaboration, and immediate visual feedback as students see their games come to life.

Students leave with more than just a game—they leave with confidence in their ability to create, problem-solve, and work together. Many discover passions they never knew they had, and teachers often report increased engagement in other subjects following participation in Build Day.

The program is completely free for participating schools and includes all necessary materials, professional development resources, and ongoing support. We provide dedicated educator guides that align with your existing curriculum standards, making it easy to integrate Build Day into your educational program. Whether you're looking to enhance your STEM offerings, provide enrichment opportunities, or simply give students a unique learning experience, Build Day delivers measurable educational outcomes while students have the time of their lives.

The Educational Value

Why game development matters in education

🧠 Cognitive Development

Game development exercises multiple cognitive skills simultaneously:

  • Executive Function: Planning, organizing, and managing complex projects
  • Spatial Reasoning: Working in 3D space enhances mathematical thinking
  • Logical Thinking: Understanding cause-and-effect through scripting
  • Critical Analysis: Testing, debugging, and iterating on designs

💬 Communication Skills

Students practice essential communication throughout the event:

  • Articulation: Explaining their design choices and game mechanics
  • Collaboration: Negotiating roles and sharing creative vision
  • Presentation: Showcasing their work to peers and mentors
  • Feedback: Giving and receiving constructive criticism

🌟 Social-Emotional Learning

Build Day naturally develops SEL competencies:

  • Growth Mindset: Learning that abilities develop through dedication
  • Resilience: Persisting through challenges and debugging errors
  • Empathy: Designing experiences for other players
  • Self-Confidence: Seeing tangible results of their learning

🔬 STEM Skills

Students gain practical STEM experience:

  • Programming: Introduction to Lua scripting and computational thinking
  • Mathematics: Applied geometry, coordinates, and physics
  • Engineering: Design process, testing, and optimization
  • Technology: Digital tool proficiency and software navigation

3 Essential Skills for Early Learning

Skills that matter now and for the future

1
🎨

Creative Thinking

In a rapidly changing world, creativity is one of the most valuable skills. The World Economic Forum ranks creativity as one of the top three skills needed for the future workforce. Build Day teaches students to:

  • Visualize and design original concepts from imagination to implementation
  • Experiment with different solutions and learn that there are multiple paths to success
  • Think critically about user experience and how to engage and delight players
  • Express themselves through digital creation using professional-grade tools
  • Connect creativity with technical skills to bring their visions to life
  • Develop their unique voice by creating games that reflect their interests and perspectives

"Every student is already creative - we just give them the tools to express it. The transformation we see when a student realizes 'I can make this' is incredible."

— Dr. Sarah Chen, Educational Psychologist

2
🔍

Problem-Solving

Game development is essentially problem-solving in action. When students build games, they encounter real challenges that require systematic thinking and persistence. Students will:

  • Identify bugs and systematically fix them using debugging methodologies used by professional developers
  • Break large projects into smaller tasks and learn project management skills
  • Test hypotheses and iterate on designs applying the scientific method naturally
  • Learn from failures and try again developing growth mindset and resilience
  • Apply logic to achieve desired outcomes strengthening computational thinking
  • Think algorithmically by creating sequences of actions and conditional behaviors

"Every error message is a learning opportunity in disguise. Students who once gave up at the first sign of difficulty now persevere because they understand that debugging is part of the process."

— Michael Torres, 7th Grade Computer Science Teacher

3
🤝

Teamwork

Collaboration is key in school and beyond. The modern workplace requires people who can work effectively in teams, and Build Day provides authentic collaborative experiences. Build Day fosters:

  • Clear communication of ideas as students explain their game concepts and design decisions
  • Active listening and compromise when working through creative differences
  • Sharing responsibilities fairly and learning to trust team members
  • Celebrating others' contributions and recognizing that great work is rarely solo
  • Building empathy through collaboration by considering different perspectives and play styles
  • Leadership skills as students take on different roles within their teams

"Together, students create something none could build alone. The magic happens when they learn to value each person's unique strengths and ideas."

— Lisa Park, Middle School Principal

How It Works for Schools

Simple registration, maximum impact

Step 1

Register Your School

Fill out our comprehensive registration form with your school details, estimated number of participants, and any special requirements. Our team will work with you to accommodate your specific needs, whether you're bringing 20 students or 200. We'll schedule a brief orientation call to answer questions and ensure you're fully prepared.

Timeline: Register at least 2 weeks before the event for best preparation time.

Step 2

Prepare Your Students

We provide everything you need: pre-event materials, teacher guides with lesson plans, student workbooks, promotional posters, and access to our educator portal. You'll receive a comprehensive preparation packet that includes introductory activities you can do in class to get students excited and familiar with basic concepts before Build Day.

Support included: Video tutorials, printable resources, and a dedicated educator support email.

Step 3

Participate Together

On event day, your students join thousands of others in a carefully orchestrated learning experience. Teachers can supervise and observe while our experienced mentors guide the hands-on learning. You'll receive a teacher dashboard showing real-time progress, common challenges, and suggestions for supporting struggling students. It's professional development in action!

Day-of support: Technical help, mentor guidance, and educator resources available throughout.

Step 4

Celebrate & Continue

Celebrate student achievements with certificates of completion, digital badges, and showcase opportunities! We provide post-event resources for continued learning, including extension activities, advanced tutorials, and connections to ongoing Roblox education programs. Many schools use Build Day as a launching point for game development clubs, coding classes, or STEM enrichment programs.

Ongoing benefits: Alumni network, advanced resources, and invitations to future events.
